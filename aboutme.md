## Comfort zones were meant to be Broken — that’s how I approach learning and growth.

Greetings,

My name is Simoné Rossouw.  
I am currently in my final year of a BSc in Information Technology at North-West University, 
and I have never been more excited about what life as a graduate will have in store

My degree has not only strengthened my coding, technical, and time management skills, but also reshaped the way I approach life’s technical challenges. It has provided me with the foundational tools I need to begin my career with confidence.

Working hard to meet deadlines and striving for quality in all my projects has always been my main goal.

Outside of my professional work, I have enjoyed playing tennis since childhood, and more recently I’ve developed a surprising passion for padel. Drawing and painting fuel my creative side and help me balance my technical focus.

I also enjoy activities on the more extreme side, including:

- Skydiving (first time: 2022)  
- Bungee jumping (first time: 2023)  
- Rock climbing (first time: 2024)  

As you can see, I’m a bit of an adrenaline junkie. Is that truly a bad thing, though? After all, comfort zones were meant to be broken.

These activities keep me on my toes and enable me to perform under pressure.
